About Pembrokeshire Woven Coffins

Grown and woven by hand here in Wales

Pembrokeshire Woven Coffins was founded to offer families a meaningful alternative to mass-produced coffins. We believe that a coffin can be simple, beautiful, and respectful of the environment.

Each coffin is woven by hand by Olivia, an experienced basket maker and tutor, using willow grown on our small farm in Pembrokeshire. From planting and harvesting to weaving and finishing, every stage is carefully considered and carried out with care.

Our work is rooted in traditional rural craft, but guided by modern values: sustainability, transparency, and personal service. We work closely with families and funeral directors to ensure everything feels calm, straightforward, and supportive at a difficult time.

Sustainable Materials

All of our coffins are made using natural, renewable materials chosen for their beauty, strength, and environmental credentials.

Willow

Willow is an exceptionally sustainable material. It grows quickly, regenerates after harvest, and can be woven without the need for industrial processing or heavy machinery.

Our willow is grown and harvested on our Pembrokeshire farm, where soil and climate influence the colour and character of each rod. We select varieties specifically suited to coffin weaving, ensuring strength, flexibility, and a refined finish.

To meet demand and to provide specialist buff and white willow, we also work with trusted growers in Somerset who share our commitment to quality and sustainability.

Construction and Finish

Coffins are available with either an all-willow woven base or an OSB base where required by crematoria.

Each coffin is finished with natural rope handles, wooden toggles, and an unbleached cotton lining. No plastics, varnishes, or synthetic fabrics are used.

Fully biodegradable, our coffins are suitable for natural burial grounds as well as conventional burial and cremation.

Our Handmade Willow Coffins

We offer curved willow coffins in a small, carefully considered range of natural finishes.

All coffins are suitable for natural and conventional burial, as well as cremation. We supply directly to families and through partnered funeral directors.

As willow is a natural material, colour and texture will vary subtly depending on season and variety. These variations are part of what makes each coffin unique.

If you have particular preferences or would like subtle custom touches, we're always happy to discuss what's possible.

Brown Willow

A warm, earthy finish made using natural brown willow. Available with optional white willow accents.

Buff Willow

A lighter, honey-coloured coffin woven using buff willow, with the option of subtle white detailing.

Our Process

We aim to make ordering a coffin as simple and reassuring as possible.

  1. Get in touch directly or through your funeral director.
  2. We discuss size, finish, and any personal preferences.
  3. Your coffin is woven by hand, specifically for you.
  4. We deliver directly or coordinate with your funeral director.

We are always happy to answer questions and talk through options, with no obligation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are willow coffins suitable for cremation?

Yes. Our willow coffins meet crematorium requirements and can be made with an OSB base where required.

How long does a willow coffin take to make?

Our coffins usually take 9 days to make due to the need to soak the willow before weaving. We also keep a small stock of coffins read to order.

Do you supply funeral directors?

We work directly with families and with funeral directors across Pembrokeshire and Wales.

How much does a willow coffin cost?

Our coffins start from £750.

Can I plan ahead?

Yes. Many people choose to plan ahead, and we are happy to discuss options without pressure.
